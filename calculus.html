Calculus

Calculus is offered to 12th graders who have successfully completed and passed Pre-Calculus, meaning an average of a D or above.

This is a traditional course in differential and integral calculus. The course is designed to prepare students for further studies of calculus at the college level. It is less rigorous than the AP Calculus courses and does not require a student to take the AP exam at its conclusion.

Calculus Curriculum

  1. Functions
  2. Limits
  3. Continuity
  4. Differentiation
  5. Applications of the Derivative
  6. The Integral
  7. Application of the Integral
  8. Techniques of Integration

Advanced Placement Criteria

There are two AP courses available to students. There is AP Calculus AB and AP Calculus BC, BC being more challenging than AB. For a placement into the AB course, you must have either completed Pre-Calculus with an A average, or Pre-Calculus Honors with a C average minimum. You can find out more information about this course on the AP Calculus AB page. For a placement into the BC course, you must have completed Pre-Calculus Honors with a B average minimum. You can find out more information about this course on the AP Calculus BC page.
