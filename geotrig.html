Geometry and Trigonometry

This course is offered to students who have completed Algebra II. You will either take it in 10th grade or 11th grade.

Geometry is designed to teach you how to discover patterns, find areas, volumes, lengths, and angles. Trigonometry is an area of mathematics that studies the relationships of angles and sides of triangles. Both of these topics are covered in this course. You will explore more complex geometric situations, along with learning how to mathematically prove something.

Geometry and Trigonometry Curriculum

  1. Logic
  2. Proving statements in Geometry
  3. Congruence of line segments, angles and triangles
  4. Congruence based on triangles
  5. Geometric inequalities
  6. Ratio, proportion, and similarity
  7. Quadrilaterals
  8. Geometry of the circle
  9. Locus
  10. Trigonometric functions
  11. Trigonometric equations
  12. Trigonometric applications

Honors Criteria

Geometry and Trigonometry Honors covers everything Geometry and Trigonometry does, but in greater depth. It is offered to students who have completed Algebra II with an A or A+, or Algebra II Honors with a B- average minimum.
